Casalist 1 (3.5)

What's New
- True multi-user family sharing. Migrated the entire data layer from SwiftData (per-user-private) to Core Data + NSPersistentCloudKitContainer with Apple's share(_:to:) API.
- "Invite family" now creates a real CKShare on the Household record so accepted members see (and can edit) the same family, tasks, goals, chores, events, and reminders.

What's Fixed
- Recipients who accept a share invite now actually see the household's data, not just an empty record.
- "Newer version of Casalist" share errors should be gone — CloudKit Production schema fully redeployed to match the new Core Data model (CD_Household entity added, CD_household relationship on every child).

What to Test
- Install 3.5 on two phones logged into two different iCloud accounts.
- On phone A: Settings (the cog icon) to add a family member or two, then Add tile to put one chore on the leaderboard, then top-right person-plus icon to send invite via Messages or Copy Link.
- On phone B: tap the share link. Casalist should open and accept it.
- Within ~30s, the family members and chore from A should show up in B's app, and vice versa. (This is the actual proof of the rewrite.)
